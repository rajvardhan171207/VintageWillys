Willys (pronounced "Willis") was an American automobile company founded by John North Willys, renowned for developing and producing the iconic World War II-era military Jeep (MB) and subsequent civilian models (CJ). As the original manufacturer of the Willys MB, the brand became synonymous with rugged 4x4 utility, establishing the foundation for modern Jeep vehicles.
